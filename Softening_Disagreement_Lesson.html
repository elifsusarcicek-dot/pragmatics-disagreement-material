🛡️
Disagreeing can feel like an attack

When you tell someone "you're wrong," you bump into their wish to look smart and be respected. Linguists call this their face. Disagreeing is a face-threatening act — it can hurt, even when you're right.

🧸
Softeners are little cushions

Words like maybe, I think, a bit, could we, I see your point but… are softeners. They wrap the disagreement in a cushion so it lands gently instead of hitting hard.

⚖️
How soft? It depends on the situation

Three things decide how careful to be: how close you are to the person, who has more power, and how big the disagreement is. A tiny disagreement with a best friend? Be direct. A big one with a teacher? Cushion it a lot.
